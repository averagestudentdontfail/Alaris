Detailed Description

Implements the Leung & Santoli (2014) pre-earnings announcement implied volatility model. The model accounts for scheduled earnings jumps in option pricing by incorporating a deterministic-time random jump in the stock price dynamics. The pre-EA implied volatility follows: I(t; K, T) = sqrt(sigma^2 + sigma_e^2 / (T - t)) Where: sigma = base (diffusion) volatility sigma_e = earnings jump volatility (calibrated from historical EA moves) T = option expiration time t = current time (must be before EA date T_e) Reference: "Accounting for Earnings Announcements in the Pricing of Equity Options" Tim Leung & Marco Santoli (2014), Journal of Derivatives.
