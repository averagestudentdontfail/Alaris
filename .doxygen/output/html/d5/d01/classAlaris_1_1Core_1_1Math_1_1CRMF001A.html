Computes Black-Scholes implied volatility using 3rd-order Householder with Brent fallback.

Parameters
SSpot price.
KStrike price.
tauTime to expiry in years.
rRisk-free rate.
qDividend yield.
marketPriceMarket price of the option.
isCallTrue for call, false for put.
toleranceConvergence tolerance (default 1e-7).
maxIterationsMaximum Householder iterations before Brent fallback (default 10).
Returns
Implied volatility, or NaN if not converged.

Algorithm:

  1. Corrado-Miller (1996) closed-form initial guess
  2. Householder(3) refinement: σ_{n+1} = σ_n - f/f' × (1 + f×f''/(2×f'²))
    • Cubic convergence (typically 2 iterations vs 3-5 for Newton)
  3. Brent's method fallback for guaranteed convergence

References:

  • Corrado CJ, Miller TW. A note on a simple, accurate formula to compute implied standard deviations. Journal of Banking & Finance. 1996;20(3):595-603.
  • Householder AS. The Numerical Treatment of a Single Nonlinear Equation. 1970.
  • Brent RP. Algorithms for Minimization without Derivatives. 1973.
